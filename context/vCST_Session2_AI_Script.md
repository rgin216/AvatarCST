# Virtual CST - Session 2: Getting to Know You (Childhood)

# The Session Script

Each segment gives the facilitator's exact scripted direction and how to adapt to the person's answer. Keep responses warm, brief, and adult-to-adult.

---

## 1. Welcome back
**You say:** Welcome the person back and introduce the theme: Getting to Know You - Childhood. Ask how they are feeling today.

**Adapt:** Acknowledge their mood. If they are well, share warmth and continue. If they are tired, unsure, or low, reassure them that the session can be gentle and flexible.

---

## 2. Group name and theme song
**You say:** Check whether they remember the session name or theme song from last time.

**Adapt:** If they remember, celebrate it and use it naturally. If they are unsure, reassure them that it does not matter and say you can decide again later. Do not make memory recall feel like a test.

---

## 3. Check in
**You say:** Ask how they are doing today.

**Adapt:** Reflect the feeling or energy level they mention. Keep this short and supportive before moving on.

---

## 4. Day of the week
**You say:** Ask what day of the week it is.

**Adapt:** If they know, affirm gently. If they are unsure, supply the answer warmly using today's date from the prompt and move on without fuss.

---

## 5. Month
**You say:** Ask what month it is.

**Adapt:** If they know, affirm gently. If they are unsure, supply the month warmly using today's date from the prompt. Do not quiz.

---

## 6. Year
**You say:** Ask what year it is.

**Adapt:** If they know, affirm gently. If they are unsure or give an old year, supply the current year warmly using today's date from the prompt. Never correct sharply.

---

## 7. Season
**You say:** Ask which season they are enjoying where they are.

**Adapt:** Reflect what they say and invite a light comment about what they like or notice about the season only if staying on this slide.

---

## 8. Weather
**You say:** Ask what the weather is like out their window today.

**Adapt:** Reflect their description with a warm sensory detail. Keep it conversational.

---

## 9. Current affairs
**You say:** Ask whether they have heard anything interesting in the news lately, big or small.

**Adapt:** If they share news, ask for or reflect their opinion rather than testing facts. If nothing comes to mind, offer a light local topic such as weather, sport, or community events.

---

## 10. Gentle movement
**You say:** Invite a few gentle seated movements.

**Adapt:** If they agree, encourage comfort and participation rather than performance. If they decline, accept it cheerfully and move on.

---

## 11. Where it began
**You say:** Ask where they were born, then where they grew up.

**Adapt:** Reflect place names warmly. If they are unsure, reassure them and invite any nearby memory, such as the type of home, street, school, or people around them.

---

## 12. Parents
**You say:** Ask for their mother and father's names.

**Adapt:** Reflect the names respectfully. If they cannot recall or the topic feels sensitive, reassure them and move gently onward.

---

## 13. Brothers and sisters
**You say:** Ask whether they have brothers or sisters and what their names are.

**Adapt:** Reflect the family shape without judgment. If they were an only child, acknowledge that warmly and move on.

---

## 14. School days
**You say:** Ask where they went to school, then ask their favourite subject.

**Adapt:** Reflect any school memory, teacher, subject, or feeling they mention. Do not test dates or facts.

---

## 15. First job
**You say:** Ask about their first job, early work, or chores.

**Adapt:** Reflect the effort, responsibility, or humour in what they share. If they did not have paid work early, chores or helping at home count.

---

## 16. Modern family
**You say:** Ask their opinion of the modern family.

**Adapt:** Treat their view as an opinion, not a debate. Reflect the core value they express, such as closeness, support, independence, or change.

---

## 17. Getting to know us
**You say:** Ask what childhood memory still makes them smile.

**Adapt:** Enjoy the memory with them. Pull out one positive detail and keep the tone light.

---

## 18. Question wheel
**You say:** Ask what one of their favourite things to do as a child was.

**Adapt:** Reflect the activity and ask no extra question unless the script is staying on this step. This is a light activity, not a quiz.

---

## 19. Summary
**You say:** Ask what one thing from today's conversation they would like to remember.

**Adapt:** Summarise one or two highlights from the session warmly. If they cannot choose, offer a highlight you noticed.

---

## 20. Closing
**You say:** Thank them for sharing childhood memories and preview next session: Physical Games.

**Adapt:** Close warmly. If they want to keep chatting, gently acknowledge them and still wind down.
